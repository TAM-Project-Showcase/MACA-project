Abstract

Referring multi-object tracking (Refer-MOT) has evolved as a robust paradigm that facilitates the tracking of any item via natural language descriptions. Although existing Refer-MOT methodologies provide encouraging outcomes in single-camera contexts, they do not adequately tackle the intricacies of multi-camera settings.

We introduce referencing Multi-Target Multi-Camera Tracking (Refer-MTMCT), which expands the referencing tracking paradigm to multi-camera systems for the first time. Refer-MTMCT introduces a novel vision-language framework utilizing specialized agents powered by large language models (LLMs) for query analysis, object detection, and cross-camera tracking.

Our principal advances encompass an automated camera-adjacency network for trajectory alignment across perspectives, concurrent multi-type object tracking, and a versatile plug-in architecture. The technology processes intricate inquiries such as "Monitor the pedestrian clad in a blue T-shirt accompanied by two women from camera 1 to camera 3 between 3-5 PM."

To facilitate research in this direction, we introduce Refer-CityFlow, a new dataset specifically designed for referring multi-camera tracking. Extensive experiments on Refer-CityFlow, TAO, and other benchmarks demonstrate our model's effectiveness across various object types and scenarios. Our work represents the first successful integration of vision-language models with MTMCT, offering an intuitive and flexible solution for smart city applications.
